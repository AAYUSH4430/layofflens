Early Warning Signs

Is Your Company
About to Cut?

These are the real signals that companies show before announcing layoffs. Check the ones you're seeing at your workplace.

✓

Hiring freeze announced

Open roles suddenly disappear or are put "on hold indefinitely" — classic pre-layoff signal.

✓

Senior leaders quietly leaving

When VPs and Directors start leaving without clear successors, they know something you don't.

✓

Budget cuts across teams

Travel, software subscriptions, team events suddenly cancelled — money is being saved for something.

✓

Unusual all-hands or town halls

Emergency meetings with vague "strategic realignment" language from leadership. Watch for this one.

✓

Performance reviews suddenly intensified

Companies often build a paper trail before cuts. New KPIs, PIPs, and reviews out of nowhere.

✓

Revenue miss or investor pressure

A bad earnings call or activist investor involvement almost always precedes workforce reduction.

✓

Your manager avoids 1:1s

When managers go quiet and become evasive, they're often under NDA about incoming changes.

✓

Consultants or "efficiency experts" arrive

McKinsey, Bain, or similar firms walking around the office is rarely a good sign for headcount.
